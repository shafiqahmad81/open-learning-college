Our user-friendly interface makes it simple to upload and manage your course materials, using our FCB (Frontend Course Builder) platform, while our advanced analytics and reporting tools provide you with valuable insights via the educators dashboard into your students’ progress and performance.

If you do not have your own course materials but would like to become an educator with Open Learning College, we can offer a dynamic and empowering experience. Additionally, our marketing team is dedicated to promoting your course to potential students, helping you to reach a wider audience and grow your business. We are committed to providing you with the resources and support you need to succeed, so you can focus on what you do best – teaching and inspiring others.

How Are Educators Paid?

Broadly speaking, we pay our educators in three ways.

  • If you own your own content and upload this to our V.O.I.C.E. platform, then for every student that enrols we will pay you up to 60% of the enrolment fee per student.
  • If you use our content and deliver it through our platform you will be paid up to 30% for every student who completes the course.
  • If you provide only tutor support we will pay you based on the number of assignments marked, thus the more help you give our students, the more you get paid.
  • Please note that our educators are all self-employed, and the contract is for services provided by the educator to us.

AUTHOR AGREEMENT

Authors

What We Require In An Author

Authors are required to write new courses or, more often, to revise and update existing courses. Specifications change regularly and it is necessary to adapt existing materials for new purposes. In addition, we have a policy of continuous improvement for all courses.

See the sample written or video course module in the resource table, this is typically the standard of material required, our content creation team will take the written or video module and build all the additional learning resources from the glossary to the module summary.

Experience & Skills

Authors must be subject specialists with a number of years experience of teaching their subject, also some experience as a published writer. Familiarity with the latest specifications is expected. Content creation and page-layout skills are also an asset.

Experience in developing courses for an online environment (LMS) is also valuable.

How Are Authors Paid?

Authors are employed on a freelance basis to undertake specific commissions for a set fee. The fee is negotiated between the author and our editorial department.

Open Learning College retains the copyright on all the developed materials.

We welcome expressions of interest from specialist authors in all the academic subjects we currently offer and fields of learning we may not already be practicing in.

Authors & Educators

An Author and Educator at Open Learning College is a versatile professional who excels in crafting accredited courses of high standards. As an Author, they leverage their expertise to develop comprehensive and engaging course content that meets educational standards of our awarding bodies. This individual is not only a content creator but also an active participant in the educational process.

In their role as an Educator, they go beyond the traditional boundaries, serving as a tutor or instructor. Whether guiding students through our Learner Management System (LMS), leading virtual sessions on Zoom, or fostering immersive learning experiences in the Metaverse, they bring the course to life. This dual role allows them to not only create educational content but also actively participate in its delivery, ensuring a seamless and impactful learning journey for students.

We can employ on a full or part-time basis or as a freelancer, to undertake specific commissions for a set fee. The freelancer fee is negotiated between the author/educator and our editorial department.

Open Learning College retains the copyright on all the developed materials.

We welcome expressions of interest from specialist authors in all the academic subjects we currently offer and fields of learning we may not already be practicing in.
